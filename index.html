Eduardo Perez

Welcome to my website. I'm an engineering student at Illinois Tech pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering. I'm currently looking for internships in the Automotive and Robotics industry. Here are my areas of Interest.

Automotive

Like other car enthusiasts, my love for cars started with my Dad's 1996 Chevy Silverado Cheyenne 2500. This truck was part of my childhood. He bought it brand new and used it ever since. But because of his stroke, the truck was parked in the garage for about 9 years. Later, my family saved up some money and bought my car. To save money on car repairs and general maintenance, I started to wrench on my car. Through this, I gained a better understanding on how cars function. Most of my car knowledge comes from my Dad, his books of automotive encyclopedia, and YouTube.

After gaining experience from fixing my car, myself, my brother, and my dad decided to bring the truck back to life. After changing the fuel pump, battery, and added 5 gallons of gas, it started up as if nothing happened. The sound of the small block v8 roaring again from sleeping put a smile on our faces. All 8 cylinders were firing and the transmission still worked! To me and my brother, we were surprised that it was still working after just sitting for a long period. But to my Dad, it was to no surprised as he was confident in his truck. Fixing the truck made me realize that I enjoy working with my hands and I gained an interest to pursue a path in the automotive industry.

Robotics

When it comes to Robotics, I start geeking out. Growing up watching NOVA and learning about the Mar's Rover during middle school, it fascinated me on how NASA was able to control or program a robot to perform different tasks on a different planet. But in science fiction through different mediums such as video games (Titanfall 2), and movies (The sound of the heavy metallic steps of an AT-AT) revealed on how creative we can get when designing robots whether for entertainment or helping the world.

This made me pursue my education into engineering. In Spring 2018, I was invited to be a volunteer in the electrical team of IIT's Nasa Robotics Mining team. This gave me firsthand experience on what it is like to build a robot. I can easily say, it is "easier said than done". I started out by helping to make wires for electrical components and assisted with troubleshooting electrical problems. Some nights I stayed with the team until midnight to prep the robot for competition. Seeing the robot come to life reminded me that NASA engineers are doing something similar in prepping their space probes and other equipment that are to be launch into space. Now that I'm part of thte team, I'm currently designing a PCB for power delievery for Low and High voltage which hopefully become a motherboard for the robot being built. Unfortunately, I wasn't able to attend the Spring 2018 Mining competition because I joined the team late. But I'm looking forward to attend the Spring 2019 competition. In the wide field of robotics, I'm interested in mechs and exoskeletons.

MakerSpace

I was introduced to a Maker Space called Pump Station One when I went with a friend to work on a wind turbine project for a club at my Community College. He was a member of the Maker Space and he showed me around the site. It amazed me of all the tools that were at the readily available to create stuff. The site had tools such for wood works, welding, 3D printing, laser cutters, electronics, and they even had an electron microscope (it is no longer there). When I was at pump station one, everyone was welcoming, and they helped each other working on their own projects.

I remember helping someone to cut open a transformer so that he can use the internal components for his Diy speaker.
